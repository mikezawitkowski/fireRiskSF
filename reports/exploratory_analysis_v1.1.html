Fire Risk - Exploratory Analysis

Load Data

Download fire incident data from 2003 onwards from the below url and load the data

https://data.sfgov.org/Public-Safety/Fire-Incidents/wr8u-xric

Data cleanup

  • There are duplicate rows for a lot of incidents. They have been consolidated

  • Incidents have been consoldated into types based on National Fire Incident Reporting System (NFIR) definitions

Some insights

1. Exploring types of incidents

  • Majority of the incidents are false alarms, followed by

  • Rescue, EMS and other service calls and then

  • Fires

A recent report shows that SF Fire Dept is spending $100,000 a day on overtime.

http://www.nbcbayarea.com/news/local/San-Francisco-Fire-Shells-Out-100000-a-Day-on-Overtime-Amid-Staffing-Shortage-387047391.html

The false alarms seem to add a lot to the workload. It would be interesting to see what kind of educational and other initiatives the city undertakes to reduce the number of false alarms.

2. Trend of all fire incidents by year

  • Number of fire incidents have been steady over the last decade with a dip in 2014

3. Trend of fire incidents by month

  • There is a spike in average number of incidents in Jan

  • The distribution for the rest of the year seems to be fairly even

4. Fire incidents by neighborhood

  • Mission and Tenderloin have the most number of fires per year
